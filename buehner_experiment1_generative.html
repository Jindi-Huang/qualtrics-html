Instructions

In this part, you will play the role of a microbiologist studying how different types of ray exposure influence the mutation of viruses.

Imagine that you are a microbiologist working in a research laboratory. Your task is to study how exposure to various types of rays influences whether viruses mutate.

You will review laboratory records from experiments conducted on petri dishes containing viruses. In each experiment:

  • Some petri dishes were exposed to rays
  • Other petri dishes were not exposed to the rays (control group)
  • You will see whether the viruses in each dish mutated or not

For each type of ray, you will see 16 laboratory records (8 exposed dishes and 8 unexposed dishes), presented one at a time.

After viewing all records for a ray type, you will be asked to judge:

  1. How strongly the rays cause mutation
  2. How confident you are in your judgment
  3. Your estimates of mutation rates in each group

You will evaluate 3 different types of rays and their effects on 3 different viruses.
