Vaccine Effectiveness Study

Welcome

Thank you for participating in this study on causal reasoning.

In this experiment, you will play the role of a virologist testing several new vaccines against various viruses.

The experiment will take approximately 45 minutes to complete.

Instructions

Imagine that you are a virologist working in a research laboratory. Your task is to test several new vaccines that have been developed to prevent various virus diseases.

You will review laboratory records from experiments conducted on rats. In each experiment:

  • Some rats received a vaccination prior to virus exposure
  • Other rats did not receive the vaccination (control group)
  • You will see whether each rat developed the disease or not

For each vaccine, you will see 16 laboratory records (8 vaccinated rats and 8 unvaccinated rats), presented one at a time.

After viewing all records for a vaccine, you will be asked to judge:

  1. How strongly the vaccine prevents the disease
  2. How confident you are in your judgment
  3. Your estimates of disease rates in each group

You will evaluate 3 different vaccines for 3 different viruses.
